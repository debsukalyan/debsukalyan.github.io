A simple 30-day roadmap

Rule: Do one small thing every day. Neuroscience feels huge because it connects biology, psychology, medicine, AI, engineering, and philosophy. You only need one entry point.
Days 1–3

Get hooked

Watch 2–3 episodes of VSauce Mind Field. Write down 5 questions that genuinely excite you.

Days 4–7

Build vocabulary

Read BrainFacts Core Concepts. Learn these words: neuron, synapse, circuit, plasticity, neurotransmitter, cortex, hippocampus, amygdala, prefrontal cortex.

Week 2

Pick a branch

Choose one: cognitive neuroscience, molecular/cellular neuroscience, computational neuroscience, neuroimaging, neurotech/BCI, clinical neuroscience, consciousness science.

Week 3

Do a mini project

Examples: summarize one paper, analyze a small open dataset, build a reaction-time experiment, explain one brain region in a reel, or make a concept map of one disorder.

Week 4

Convert curiosity into direction

Find 5 labs working on your chosen topic. Read their recent work. Make a one-page note: what they study, methods they use, skills needed, and why you care.

What to learn based on your background

Psychology

Your advantage

Behavior, cognition, emotion, experiments.

  • Add: statistics, coding, neuroanatomy, fMRI/EEG basics
  • Best fit: cognitive neuroscience, clinical research, decision-making, social neuroscience
Biology

Your advantage

Cells, molecules, physiology, genetics.

  • Add: systems thinking, behavior, coding/data analysis
  • Best fit: molecular, cellular, developmental, neurogenetics, systems neuroscience
Engineering / CS

Your advantage

Coding, math, signals, models, systems.

  • Add: basic neurobiology, experimental design, statistics
  • Best fit: computational neuroscience, BCI, neurotech, AI for brain data
