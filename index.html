About Me

I am a cognitive neuroscience researcher working at the intersection of emotion, cognition, and motivation. As part of my research in the Emotion and Cognition Lab, IISc Bangalore, I design behavioural tasks, collect data, and analyse both behaviour and brain activity (fMRI) to understand how reward and motivation shape cognitive control of emotional conflict.

An allied interest of mine is applying behavioural science to solve real-world problems — especially in marketing and consumer behaviour: how attention, reward expectation, and perceived threat/safety change decisions in the wild.

Research Focus

Cognitive control of emotional conflict: How proactive control mechanisms resolve affective interference in the brain and behaviour.

Emotion × Control

How the brain exerts control when emotional stimuli try to hijack attention, and how this shows up in behaviour and accuracy under conflict.

Motivation & Reward

How reward expectation drives proactive control: can motivation prepare the system in advance to resist distraction?

Uncertainty

How uncertainty in expected outcomes (probability/variance of reward) changes control allocation and decision dynamics, modelled with hierarchical DDMs.
